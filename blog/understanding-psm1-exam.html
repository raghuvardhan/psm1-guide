Understanding the PSM-1 Exam: A Comprehensive Guide

By Your Name | Published on January 15, 2024

The Professional Scrum Master I (PSM-1) certification is a globally recognized credential that validates your understanding of the Scrum framework and its application. Whether you're new to Scrum or looking to formalize your knowledge, passing the PSM-1 exam is a significant step in your Agile journey.

What is the PSM-1 Exam?

The PSM-1 exam, offered by Scrum.org, assesses your knowledge of Scrum theory, practices, roles, events, and artifacts as defined in the Scrum Guide. The exam consists of 80 multiple-choice questions to be completed in 60 minutes, with a passing score of 85%.

Key Topics Covered

  • Scrum Theory and Principles
  • The Scrum Framework
  • Scrum Roles and Responsibilities
  • Scrum Events and Artifacts
  • Scaling Scrum

Preparation Strategies

To pass the PSM-1 exam on your first attempt, consider the following strategies:

  1. Study the Scrum Guide Thoroughly: The Scrum Guide is the foundation of the exam. Understand every concept and practice described.
  2. Use Practice Exams: Familiarize yourself with the exam format and question styles by taking practice tests.
  3. Join Scrum Communities: Engage with others preparing for the exam to exchange knowledge and tips.
  4. Invest in Quality Study Materials: Utilize comprehensive guides that offer summaries, practice questions, and exam tips.
